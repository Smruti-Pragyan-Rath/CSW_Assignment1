S = vᵢt + ½at²: The Equation of Linear Motion

The formula S = vᵢt + ½at² describes the motion of an object under constant acceleration. Here:

  • S represents the displacement,
  • vis the initial velocity,
  • t s the time, ,
  • a is the acceleration, and
  • ½at² is the additional displacement due to acceleration.

"An object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force." Note: This equation was originally written in 1905.
